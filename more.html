National Conference

  • 2010

    Dr. B.R. Ambedkar Open University, Hydrabad was local host and Indian Academy of Social Sciences, Allahbad, was organizer of XXXIII Social Science Congress which was held on 10-14 March 2010. The focal theme of this congress was ‘Our Planet in Crisis’ The title of research paper which was read ‘Manviya Asmita Ke Sandarbh Mein Shiksha : Chunautiyan Evam Sambhavnayen’

  • 2010

    University Grant Commission, New Delhi was funding agency and Department of Political Science, Dayanand Girls P.G. College, Kanpur (U.P.) was host of this National Seminar. The main theme of this seminar was ‘Right to Information Act. Challenges and Role in Present Context’. This seminar was held on 9th December 2010. The title of presented research paper was ‘Right to information & Human Rights’.

  • 2011

    54th Annual Conference of Indian Political Science Association on ‘Emerging Nature of Indian Politics’ was held on 28-30 December 2011. The local host was deptt. of Public Administration, University of Lucknow. The title of read research paper was ‘Bhartiya Samvaidhanik Vyavastha Ke Rajneetik Dvandva : Ek Vishleshan’

  • 2011

    Christ Church College, Kanpur (U.P.) was local host of U.G.C. New Delhi sponsored National Conference which was scheduled on 3-5 November 2011. The main theme of this seminar was ‘Judicial Activism in India Protects Challenges and Treats’. ‘Samvidhan Ke Paripeksha Mein Nyaipalika Ke Kshetradhikar Ka Prashna’ was the title of sent research paper.

  • 2011

    A National Workshop was attended on ‘ICT : An Effective Tool For Qualitative Improvement In Higher Education’ was organized by Govt. College, Aron (M.P.). This workshop was sponsored by UGC C.R.O., Bhopal (M.P.). This workshop was scheduled on 10-11 October 2011.

New Delhi was attended and read a research paper entitled ‘Bharatvarsha Mein Samaveshi Loktantra Ka Prashan : Chunautiyan Evam Sambhavnayen’.This Social Science Congress was scheduled on 27-28 February and 1st March 2014 and hosted by Giri Institute of Development Studies, Lucknow (U.P.)
